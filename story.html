MY STORY


Growing up, I was always shy and hesitant to step outside my comfort zone, convinced that it was better to be safe than sorry. My mindset changed during a college semester abroad when I found myself alone in a bustling marketplace with a map I couldn't read and a phrasebook that offered little help. I was overwhelmed and on the verge of giving up, but the friendly smile of a street vendor selling fresh fruit gave me the courage to try. With a mix of broken phrases and hand gestures, I not only bought the sweetest mango I've ever tasted but also learned that vulnerability is often the first step toward genuine human. That day taught me that the best stories are found just beyond the safe, well-marked path, and I've been seeking them ever since


To overcome my introverted personality, I had to stop seeing it as a weakness and start understanding it as a preference . For years, I believed I was at a disadvantage in a world that rewarded being outgoing and outspoken. I was the child who preferred the quiet corner with a book over the bustling playground, the student who dreaded speaking in class, and the employee who faded into the background during meetings. This created a cycle of anxiety and low confidence that held me back from connecting with others and achieving my goals.


My turning point came from a conscious decision to challenge my comfort zone, not to become an extrovert, but to develop the communication skills I lacked. I started small, pushing myself to initiate brief, low-stakes conversations. I joined a new club focused on one of my passions, which gave me a comfortable environment to practice speaking. My fear of being judged gradually faded as I realized that focusing on others' passions and asking open-ended questions made interactions less about me and more about a shared connection. Over time, these small acts of courage built a foundation of confidence. While I still cherish my solitude to recharge, I now have the ability to engage with the world on my own terms, not out of obligation, but with a genuine sense of connection.
